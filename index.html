An explicit list of features

  • Mutiple Ropes: I expanded the starter rope code to support multiple swinging ropes. There is also one way interaction with spheres and collision handling logic reused from the particle simulation demo.
  • Cloth Simulation: Horizontal springs were added to the ropes to create the cloth. The video shows the cloth falling on the sphere obstacle.
  • 3D Simulation: The cloth is rendered using Unity 3D. There is also controls for the camera to change the view. arrow keys to move around and right click while dragging the mouse to look around. q and e keys to go up and down.
  • High quality rendering - The rendering uses 3D lighting provided by Unity 3D. The cloth is textured with an image of the University of Minnesota logo.
  • Challenge: Continuum Fluid simulation - The second video shows a visualization of the shallow water equation in 1D. It's expanded to a 2D mesh colored blue to look like water. There is a border around the fluid to make it look like a tank of water. The rendering is also done in Unity 3D.
